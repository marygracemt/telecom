Telecommunications, also known as telecom, is a slowly growing sector. It is a way of communicating or exchanging information over distances that can take various forms. This plays a crucial role in everyone's lives, allowing them to interact despite being miles away. This can be transferred through different mediums like electromagnetic fields, light, cable, fiber, and many more.


Its medium before was voice, but as time evolved, video, data, and text could also be sent back and forth. This can be offered by various corporations that provide services that allow individuals and businesses to do their tasks using it. This can also be done with a wide range of technologies. And this is still evolving for further improvements.

World Telecommunication and Information Society Day (WTISD) is celebrated every May 17 since 1969 which marks the anniversary of the signing of the first International Telegraph Convention and the creation of the International Telecommunication Union (ITU).


The purpose of the WTISD is to raise awareness of the possibilities that the use of the Internet and other information and communication technologies (ICTs) can bring to societies and economies, as well as of ways to bridge the digital divide (United Nations, n.d.).


With the many significant roles telecommunications has placed in our lives, this day is celebrated every year in order to spread awareness and to improve telecommunications especially in the least developed countries. Thus, their theme for this year is “Empowering the least developed countries through information and communication technologies.”
